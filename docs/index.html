What is it?

TheSlowBrain is a careful research assistant for the stock market. It reads evidence about companies, scores how promising each one looks, double-checks itself against history, and then gives you a clear suggestion. It is deliberately slow and cautious — its main job is to avoid fooling itself. It does not move your money.

To build it, the brain studied roughly 300,000 ideas across about 3,800 companies over seven years of market history (2019–2026). The quick demo on the next tab runs on a small synthetic stand-in, so you can try the whole machine without needing that data.

It suggests

You get a short, plain list of what to buy, sell, hold, watch, or avoid.

It checks itself

Every idea is tested against past data before it is trusted.

It stays honest

If the evidence is weak or missing, it says so instead of guessing confidently.

The five answers it gives

Every stock it looks at gets exactly one of these labels:

BUYstrong & passes every check SELLtime to exit a holding HOLDdo nothing for now WATCHLISTalmost — keep watching AVOIDlooks good, but a red flag blocks it

What you get back

A short brief, written to be glanceable (example shown — yours will reflect the data):

Eric - TheSlowBrain
Stocks to buy are: none
Stocks to sell are: none
Current portfolio stocks are: AAA, BBB, CCC

✅ Seeing "none to buy" is normal and healthy — the brain only speaks up when the evidence is genuinely strong.

How a decision is made

Each run flows through the same pipeline. Nothing reaches a decision without passing the guards.

Import raw evidence

Research ideas + their historical forward returns are imported with a checksum manifest. Old generated reports are never imported as truth.

Build features

Price, volume, trend, momentum and candle context — plus optional read-only market data (Alpha Vantage → Finnhub → Yahoo).

Quarantine bad data

Malformed rows and implausible penny-stock returns are flagged and excluded from decisions and tests.

Score with the rubric

An explicit, versioned rubric assigns one of the five labels.

Backtest & guard

Out-of-sample tests, costs, drawdown, and overfitting checks decide whether a better rubric is worth adopting.

Review & calibrate

An evaluation council scores quality; results are calibrated against human-verified examples.

Shadow-learn & report

A small learning model trains in the background and earns its way up a profit-graded ladder, then the brief and track record are written.

The labels, a little deeper

LabelWhen it appears
BUYHigh score that clears every quality, risk, and data gate.
SELLExit or negative signal on a name you hold.
HOLDNeutral — no meaningful edge either way.
WATCHLISTA near-miss in the band just below the buy threshold.
AVOIDA high score actively blocked by a failed gate — an explicit negative screen.

What each check looks at

Data quality

Malformed JSON, missing fields, invalid numbers, implausible returns.

Market evidence

Benchmark return, liquidity, and universe membership — honestly labelled when only a proxy is available.

Backtest statistics

Validation + separate confirmation, deflated Sharpe, overfitting probability, drawdown, after-cost returns.

Evaluation council

Profit evidence, risk control, execution safety, report honesty, economic rationale.

Human calibration

Agreement with a held-out set of human-verified labels (reported honestly, non-binding at low sample size).

Execution safety

Position sizing, cash reserve, sector caps — all order intents stay blocked by default.

The learning gate

A from-scratch scalar autograd engine (microgix, ~150 lines, pure stdlib — reverse-mode backprop, no numpy/torch) powers a small logistic gate over the five labels, trained on historical net forward returns.

Earns its way

Starts in shadow; climbs shadow → confirm_only → co_decide → gate_primary one earned rung at a time. At gate_primary the NN proposes decisions and the rubric becomes a guardrail.

Graded on profit, demotes

Promotion needs the gate to beat the rubric on after-cost, out-of-sample return (deflated-Sharpe + PBO guarded); calibration and anchor agreement are secondary checks. If its edge fades, it auto-demotes.

Held-out anchor

30 human-verified labels are scored as an anchor only — never in any training fold.

Calibration

Reports accuracy, Brier score, expected calibration error (ECE), and anchor kappa.

Statistical rigor

  • Leakage control: purged + embargoed cross-validation, and a separate confirmation holdout distinct from the validation set.
  • Deflated Sharpe ratio with a p-value, accounting for the number of trials.
  • Probability of backtest overfitting (CSCV-style) alongside skew/kurtosis of returns.
  • Multiple-testing correction on adoption significance; degenerate / sign-flip candidates are rejected.
  • Outcome quarantine: sub-$1 names with implausible forward returns are flagged error-severity and excluded from BUY/backtest/training evidence.
  • Walk-forward windows, after-cost returns, turnover, drawdown, Sortino/Calmar, and capacity flags.

Broker boundary (gated, off by default)

A manual Trading 212 path exists for completeness, but a live order is impossible unless every gate passes:

fresh ready preview process-scoped enable flag explicit --execute matching approval token valid broker health duplicate-ledger clearance post-submit reconciliation

🚫 The scheduled daily runner is shadow-only. It never sets the live flag and never calls the submit path.

Extending it safely

Stay in the pipeline

Add evidence/features upstream of scoring; never create a path that reaches a decision without backtest + eval + safety checks.

Test both layers

Anything touching money, persisted state, or branching logic needs unit and integration tests. Modules stay under ~500 lines (enforced).

Earn promotion

The learned gate gains influence only via the documented out-of-sample criteria — never by default, never because a schedule ran.

Keep it honest

Proxy/missing/low-sample/stale evidence must be labelled as such. No false proof.
